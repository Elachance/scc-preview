How We Learn

Programs at SCC

Child-initiated, teacher-guided, and rooted in the natural world. Every day is different — because every child is different.

Our Approach

Emergent Curriculum

Emergent curriculum is a philosophy of teaching that focuses on being responsive to children’s interests to create meaningful learning experiences. Rather than following a fixed syllabus, our teachers observe each child, identify sparks of curiosity, and build learning experiences around them.

This philosophy prioritizes active participation, relationship building, inquiry, and play-based learning. Our curriculum is child-initiated, collaborative, and responsive to the children’s needs. On a regular basis, teachers design interactions and activities to facilitate growth in verbal, expressive, small and gross motor skills through active exploration and positive interactions.

You won’t see worksheets at SCC. You will see children floating leaves down a water pipe, building towers with blocks, digging for wooden letters that spell their name, and watching a butterfly emerge from its chrysalis.

Year-Round Classes

Our Classrooms

Ages 2–3

Seedlings & Acorns

Our Seedlings class is for children ages 2–3 who are not yet potty trained. Our Acorns class is for children ages 2–3 who are potty trained.

Focus is on play that fosters cooperation, socialization, self-regulation, and tactile experiences. Basic skills such as counting, colors, shapes, and listening are naturally incorporated. Nap/Rest time is included for ¾ and Full Day students. In the Seedlings classroom, potty training is also supported.

Ages 4–7

Pinecones & Oak Trees

The Pinecones class (newly 4 year-olds) and Oak Trees class (ages 5+) are more structured as we prepare older children for kindergarten.

We have a stronger focus on pre-kindergarten skills: number and letter recognition, basic writing skills, listening comprehension, problem solving, conflict resolution, and continued independence skills.

A Typical Day

Daily Schedule

Every day is a new experience at SCC, guided by the children’s interests. This is the general format.

8:30 AM
Arrival & Outside Exploration
As students arrive, the day begins with outdoor free play in the yards.
9:00 AM
Circle Time
After hand washing, students gather with their teachers to discuss themes, read a story, or sing.
9:20 AM
Morning Hike
Time to explore the grounds and trails surrounding the school — Bobcat Creek, the Hawk Tree, the Stables, and beyond — looking for local flora, fauna, bugs, and small critters.
10:00 AM
Snack
Back on campus, we wash hands and sit down for a healthy snack provided by the school.
10:20 AM
Outside Exploration
There’s always something new to discover in our play yards — teachers guide activities to inspire little minds.
11:50 AM
Lunchtime
After cleanup and hand washing, it’s lunchtime. Lunches are provided by families in reusable containers. (No heating of food.)
12:20 PM
Group Story
Students gather for story time as ½ day families prepare for pickup.
12:30 PM
Pickup (½ Day) / Rest Time (¾ & Full Day)
Half-day students head home. ¾ and Full Day students rest; non-nappers enjoy quiet activities — puzzles, books, drawing.
2:30 PM
Wake & Outside Exploration
Back outside as ¾ day students await pickup.
3:00 PM
Pickup (¾ Day) / Afternoon Snack (Full Day)
¾ day students head home. Full day students enjoy an afternoon snack.
3:45 PM
Afternoon Hike
A perfect way to get some afternoon energy out.
5:00 PM
Pickup (Full Day)
See you tomorrow!
