Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything families want to know before their first visit to the canyons.

What will my child learn at SCC? How do academics play a role?

Academics are incorporated into all we do, utilizing everyday activities — counting rocks, identifying trees and wildlife, seeing patterns in nature, and more.

Letter recognition and the alphabet are naturally integrated into daily play ("what letter does the word 'bird' start with?") and educational songs are woven into circle time activities.

SEM lessons happen organically — that's "STEM" without the "T," as we are a technology-free school. Floating leaves down a water pipe, building towers with blocks, and constructing obstacle courses with crates and bridges all teach early concepts in physics and math. The natural world offers continuous opportunities for learning. Teacher-led activities, appropriate for each classroom level, complement the child-led exploration that happens every day.

While you won't see worksheets on our campus, you'll see plenty of opportunities for learning — from digging in the sensory bin for wooden letters that spell your name, to observing the life cycle of butterflies through metamorphosis.

Our school's values are always aligned: foremost, we are here to observe, respect, and support each child's inquiries. We approach a child's interest in literacy the same way we approach an interest in painting — we observe, document, reflect, collaborate, and support continued exploration.

Our teachers treat each child as an individual, respect their level of development, and do everything possible to promote growth for each one.

How are lunches and snack time handled?

Nutritious mid-morning and mid-afternoon snacks are provided daily — at 10 AM and 3 PM for full-day students. The menu varies each day: fresh fruit, vegetables and dip, pita chips and hummus, cereal, applesauce, cheese and crackers, chips and salsa, and similar options. All snacks meet the nutritional standards required by California Childcare Licensing.

Student allergies are monitored closely to ensure no food allergens appear on the menu.

Children must bring their own lunch, served at 12 PM. We do not provide refrigeration or the ability to warm up food — lunches must be ready to serve straight from their lunchbox, which is kept in a cooler through the morning.

Do you offer extended hours — early drop-off or late pickup?

We are not able to offer extended hours. Our earliest drop-off is 8:30 AM and latest pickup is 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday.

What is your teacher-to-student ratio?

  • Seedlings: 1:6 ratio (1 Lead Teacher and 1 Assistant Teacher to 12 children per day)
  • Acorns: no more than 1:10
  • Pinecones & Oak Trees: no more than 1:12

How much is tuition?

Please visit our Enrollment page for current tuition details.

A 5% discount is available to families in the 92676 zip code.

Do you have tuition assistance available?

Yes. SCC partners with Children's Home Society of California, which offers financial aid to families who qualify. Learn more at chs-ca.org.

What immunizations are required prior to enrollment?

California requires all children enrolled in preschool to be up to date on their immunizations. Please visit our Vaccines page for the complete list of required immunizations and information about medical exemptions.

Please note: this applies to both medical exemptions and delayed schedules. A doctor's note is not sufficient — exemptions must be recorded in the CAIR-ME website.

Do you have a waiting list?

We do not currently have a waiting list.

As a year-round school, our classroom availability changes constantly. Students move between classrooms based on developmental milestones — not school-year calendars. Once you complete an inquiry, we'll share the most current enrollment availability for your child's age group.

Are you a nut-free school?

At this time we do not have any students enrolled with a peanut allergy, so we are not a nut-free school. If that changes, we will notify all enrolled families and update our policy accordingly.

My child is almost 2 — can they start early?

Our school is licensed for children ages 2–6. We are able to enroll students starting on their 2nd birthday, up until the day before they turn 7.
